<p><p>The flavor system market is categorized into three main types: natural flavors, nature-identical flavors, and artificial flavors. Natural flavors are derived from plant or animal sources, providing authentic taste profiles. Nature-identical flavors are synthetically produced to mimic natural flavors but have identical chemical structures. Artificial flavors are entirely man-made and do not occur in nature, designed to create specific taste experiences. Each type serves different needs in various industries, including food, beverages, and pharmaceuticals, catering to consumer preferences and regulatory requirements.</p></p>
