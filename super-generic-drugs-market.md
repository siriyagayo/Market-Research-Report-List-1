<p><p>The super generic drugs market consists of two main types: traditional generic drugs and biosimilars. Traditional generic drugs are chemically identical versions of branded medications, produced after the original patent expires, typically leading to cost savings for consumers. Biosimilars, on the other hand, are biologic products that are highly similar to an already approved reference biologic, but may have slight differences in their structure or formulation. Both categories aim to enhance patient access to affordable treatments while maintaining therapeutic efficacy.</p></p>
