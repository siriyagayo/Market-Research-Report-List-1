<p><ul><li>Lump Charcoal</li><li>Japanese Charcoal (Black Charcoal and White Charcoal)</li><li>Extruded Charcoal</li><li>Charcoal Briquette</li><li>Sugar Charcoal</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The industrial and barbecue charcoal market includes various types tailored for different uses. Lump charcoal is natural wood charred to retain flavor, ideal for grilling. Japanese charcoal consists of black and white varieties; black is dense and produces minimal smoke, while white is lighter with a high burn temperature. Extruded charcoal is formed under pressure for consistent shape and burn. Charcoal briquettes combine charcoal with binders for longer burn times, while sugar charcoal utilizes sugarcane for eco-friendly grilling options.</p></p>
