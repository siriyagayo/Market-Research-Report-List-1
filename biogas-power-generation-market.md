<p><p>Biogas power generation involves converting organic waste into renewable energy through two main market types: external combustion and internal combustion. In external combustion, biogas is burned in a separate boiler or gas turbine to generate steam or drive turbines, producing electricity. In contrast, internal combustion engines directly burn biogas within the engine cylinders, powering generators for electricity production. Both methods provide sustainable energy solutions, although their efficiencies and technological complexities vary, impacting their respective market applications and growth potential.</p></p>
