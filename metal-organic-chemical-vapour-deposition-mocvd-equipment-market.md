<p><p>The Metal Organic Chemical Vapour Deposition (MOCVD) equipment market features three primary heating types: RF Heating, Infrared Radiation Heating, and Resistance Heating. RF Heating uses radio frequency energy to excite gas molecules, promoting uniform deposition. Infrared Radiation Heating employs infrared light to heat the substrate directly, enabling rapid temperature increases. Resistance Heating relies on electrical resistance to generate heat, providing controlled and consistent temperatures. Each type has distinct advantages, influencing the choice based on specific application needs within semiconductor and optoelectronic manufacturing.</p></p>
