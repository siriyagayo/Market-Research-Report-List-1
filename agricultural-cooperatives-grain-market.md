<p><p>Agricultural cooperatives in the grain sector can be categorized into three main market types. Supply and Service Cooperatives provide farmers with essential inputs like seeds and fertilizers, as well as services such as equipment rental. Marketing Cooperatives focus on collectively selling members' grain to enhance market access and maximize profits. Federated Cooperatives act as a network of local cooperatives, pooling resources and bargaining power to negotiate better prices and services for their member cooperatives, ultimately strengthening their market position.</p></p>
